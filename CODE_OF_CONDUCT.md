# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want Cos to be a welcoming, harassment-free project for everyone, regardless of
background or identity. Contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation a
respectful, constructive, and professional experience.

## Our standards

Cos adopts the **[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)**
as its code of conduct. Please read the full text at that link. In short:

- **Do:** be respectful and welcoming, assume good faith, give and accept constructive
  feedback gracefully, and focus on what is best for the community.
- **Don't:** demean, insult, or personally attack others; publish other people's private
  information without permission; or otherwise behave in a way that would be
  inappropriate in a professional setting.

These standards apply in all project spaces — the repository, issues, pull requests, and
any public space where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it privately to the
maintainer:

- **LinkedIn:** message [Philip Yazdani](https://www.linkedin.com/in/philip-yazdani/) and
  start your message with `[CONDUCT]` so it is easy to triage.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer will
respect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports a concern, and will determine and
apply appropriate consequences for any behavior that violates this code.
